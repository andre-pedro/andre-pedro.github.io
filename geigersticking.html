Geiger's Ticking

Category: Videogame; September 2020 Group Project

Premise:

Geiger's Ticking tells the story of a scientist working on a nuclear powerplant through his own eyes. There are rumors of a new technology being tested on the main reactor (and some say this new technology is top-secret...). A problem occurs during one of the tests. The alarms are howling and so is your breath. You've managed to hide in a safe room, but no one has searched for you....

You're on your own. ...or are you?

My Role:

Project Management

Responsible for planning and dividing the project workload into different tasks and sprints using scheduling and organization tools (i.e. Gantt charts and Notion respectfully), as well as meeting regularly with the team to recieve feedback on the progress on a weekly basis.

Game Design

Based on the game premise, I did research on the space and time Geiger's Ticking took place in order to build historically and visually accurate scenes of a post-catastrophe powerplant in the Unity game engine, using rooms; props; lighting and vfx to engage the player in the visual storytelling (following the "show, don't tell" principle).

3D Art

Used Autodesk Maya and Substance Painter to design 3D objects from scratch (exporting both a destructed and an intact version of each), that were utilized to create an eerie and post-explosion scenario.
